I grew up in Neenah, Wisconsin.  Thanks to the success of the local foundry, Neenah is the capital of manhole covers in the United States.  Spotting a Neenah manhole cover has often reminded me of home as I was criss-crossing the country throughout my business career.

When I was a little boy, I used to love to dig in the dirt in the backyard.  I remember my Mom once teasing me: "What are you trying to do?  Dig a hole to China?"  We laughed, but I guess neither of us realized that, figuratively at least, I kept digging that hole and she was correct about where it would lead.

I'm in an introspective mood this morning.  Maybe it helps to be a stranger in a strange land.  I know being a lao ren certainly doesn't hurt.  Maybe it's because I'm a diehard idealist.  I don't know.  But I see beauty all around me--even in manhole covers.
