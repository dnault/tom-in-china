My nephew Jacob and the Boys' Choir he belongs to are heading to China in just a few weeks to perform in several Chinese cities.  So, when today I stumbled upon the explanation of Chinese currency that you can link to below, I knew I had to post it immediately.

For simplicity sake, I think of 1 renminbi (pronounced: wren-min [as in minute]-bee) as $0.14.  So you can think of a 100 renminbi bill as a $14 bill or a 1 renminbi bill as a $0.14 bill.  The coins you're most likely to encounter are the Chinese equivalent of our dime coin, that is, 1/10 of a renminbi ($0.014) or their equivalent of our half dollar coin, which is 1/2 of a renminbi ($0.07).
